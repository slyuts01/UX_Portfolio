Qualitative Research

I've been conducting semi-structured user interviews for a carbon footprint calculating app.

My goal was to find out "how people make conscious decisions to shop responsibly."

Project Brief

In a mission to create solutions that promote responsible shopping, with a focus on developing an app for comparing product carbon footprints, our project has taken its initial steps during the "empathize stage." The primary goal is to understand our potential users deeply before any design work.

My task was to find out how people make conscious decisions to shop responsibly—from which products and issues they are particularly concerned about, to where and when they find it most difficult to shop in a responsible way.

Goal

Plan, carry out and analyse user interviews to learn about your participants’ shopping habits and what’s important to them as responsible consumers.

Activities to perfom

I carried out the following activities:

1. Create an interview project plan.

2. Recruit relevant participants.

3. Carry out semi-structured user interviews.

4. Analyse my research results.

Project Planning

To achieve my task, I've undertaken a series of key activities, starting with crafting a well-thought-out interview project plan. Following this, I carefully recruited relevant participants who reflect the diversity of our potential future user base.

Equipped with a well-prepared interview guide, I conducted semi-structured user interviews, each offering distinct insights into how individuals consciously choose to shop responsibly.

Thematic Analysis

After interviewing all six participants , I listened to the recorded interviews and picked out important points or "codes" from each one. Then, I took all these codes and grouped them together based on similar ideas, forming a diagram that organised the information in a way that made it clear what the most important things were to our users. This diagram will now help guide the design work, making sure the focus is on what matters most to people when they shop responsibly.

Empathy Maps

After creating group mapping visualisations, I developed empathy maps as the next step in our user-centered design journey.

These maps delve into our users' thoughts, feelings, and motivations, fostering empathy and insight into their needs and challenges.

They also aid in crafting user personas, igniting creativity within our team and helping us validate our assumptions and research findings.

User Persona

Concluding the user research thematic analysis, I crafted two user personas, humanisng our design process and aligning it with real people's needs and preferences.

These personas serve as relatable references, ensuring our design efforts are tailored to actual user behaviors and motivations, thus reducing assumptions.

They guide decision-making, prioritise features, facilitate effective team communication, validate research findings, and reinforce our human-centric design approach, connecting our solutions with the individuals they aim to serve.

Conclusion

In conclusion, this project has been a personal favorite, as it allowed me to engage in meaningful conversations through interviews and explore a subject that resonates deeply with me. The passion I have for this topic was mirrored in the ease of recruiting interviewees, as the subject matter holds significance for many.

Next Steps

Looking ahead, our next steps involve the methodical process of triangulation , which will further validate our findings by cross-referencing them with existing research. Additionally, the aim is to broaden our understanding by venturing into quantitative research through surveys, which will enable us to collect data from a larger pool of potential users. This multifaceted approach will continue to fuel our mission to design solutions that empower responsible shopping. 🌱
