Introduction

NatWest is a UK-based bank that provides personal, private, and business banking services to over 12.5 million customers in England and Wales, and large corporates in Western Europe. With a proud history of innovation, including the launch of the Switch debit card and pioneering the growth of cash machines, NatWest is also committed to financial education through its MoneySense programme for young people. The NatWest mobile banking app is used by over 5 million customers each day, with more than 1.8 billion log-ins every year, offering features such as balance and transaction checks, quick payments, and secure messaging with the bank's 24/7 customer service.

The Challenge

So, I recently switched to NatWest bank from one of those cool fintech banks, and boy, was I in for a surprise! I got so used to having all those awesome features in my old banking app, but when I started using NatWest's app, it felt like something was missing. That got me thinking: do other NatWest users feel the same way? And can I do something about it to make the app better for all of us?

So, I decided to take matters into my own hands and set out on a little mission. The challenge was clear ‚Äì find out what's bugging other users about the NatWest Mobile banking app. I knew if I wanted to make a real difference, I needed to understand exactly what people were struggling with.

Here's what I wanted to explore:

Biometrics Hassles: You know those fingerprint and facial recognition logins? Well, I wanted to see if they were causing any headaches for folks and find out if there were any hiccups during the authentication process.

Navigational Maze: The app's navigation had me scratching my head at times. I wanted to figure out if others were getting lost too and if certain parts of the app were just not as user-friendly as they could be.

Missing Notifications: One thing that bugged me was the absence of push notifications for incoming payments. I wondered if others missed them too and if that affected their money management.

Statement Download Dilemma: So, there's no direct option to download statements in the app. I wondered how people were coping with that and if it was something they really wanted.

My goal was to use this research to propose a revamped NatWest Mobile banking app that would fix these issues. I wanted to make sure it was a breeze to use, biometrics were smooth, navigation made sense, notifications kept users in the loop, and getting statements was a piece of cake.

This case study will dig into the user research I did, spill the beans on what NatWest users had to say, and lay out my killer plan to make the app rock! I'm all about improving the app experience for everyone, and I hope this redesign will put smiles on our faces and make banking with NatWest a real pleasure. Let's do this! üöÄ

The Design Process

Empathise: In this phase, I put on my detective hat and dived deep into understanding the users' perspectives, needs, and pain points. I conducted online surveys to gather quantitative data from a wide range of NatWest app users. I also scheduled in-depth interviews with selected participants to get qualitative insights and hear their stories firsthand. By empathising with their experiences and frustrations, I could build a solid foundation for the redesign process.

Define: Armed with the research findings, I analyzed the data to identify common themes and key issues. This helped me define the specific challenges faced by users when dealing with biometrics, app navigation, incoming payment notifications, and statement downloads. By clearly defining the problems, I could narrow down my focus and prioritize the most critical aspects that needed improvement.

Ideate: The creative juices started flowing as I brainstormed potential solutions to tackle the identified challenges. I worked to generate fresh ideas and explored various design concepts that could make the NatWest Mobile banking app more user-friendly and efficient. I wanted to come up with solutions that would not only solve the issues at hand but also enhance the overall user experience.

Design / Prototype: With a clear vision in mind, I translated the ideas into concrete designs and interactive prototypes. I worked on creating a more intuitive app navigation system, a seamless biometric login experience, and a smart way to notify users about incoming payments. Additionally, I explored options for enabling statement downloads directly from the app. These designs and prototypes served as the blueprint for the revamped app.

Test / User Feedback: It was showtime! I invited NatWest app users to participate in usability testing sessions, where they interacted with the redesigned prototypes. Their feedback was invaluable in validating the effectiveness of the proposed solutions. I carefully observed how users navigated through the app, their reactions to the biometric login, and their thoughts on receiving payment notifications. I also gathered insights on the ease of statement downloads.

Throughout this process, I made sure to remain open to user feedback and made necessary iterations based on their suggestions. This iterative approach ensured that the final redesign truly catered to the needs and preferences of NatWest app users.

Now, with a solid Empathise, Define, Ideate, Design/Prototype, and Test/User Feedback process under my belt, I was ready to present the well-crafted redesign that would delight NatWest users and make their mobile banking experience a breeze!

User research

To improve the Natwest Mobile banking app, I conducted user research to identify pain points and gather insights from current users. The research was guided by the following objectives:

Objective 1: Understand Biometrics Usage Issues

The first objective aimed to gain a deeper understanding of users' experiences with biometric login (fingerprint or facial recognition). By exploring any challenges they faced during the authentication process, I sought to uncover specific issues that hindered the smooth use of biometrics.

Objective 2: Identify Navigation Complexity

The second objective focused on app navigation. I wanted to identify areas where users found the app's navigation system complex or unintuitive. By pinpointing these pain points, I aimed to improve the overall user journey and make navigation more seamless.

Objective 3: Gather Insights on Incoming Payment Notifications

The third objective centered around incoming payment notifications. I aimed to understand the importance of push notifications for users and explore how the lack of such notifications might impact their ability to manage transactions effectively.

Objective 4: Explore Statements Download Process

The fourth objective delved into the process of downloading statements from the app. I aimed to uncover users' current methods for accessing statements and gauge their feelings about the lack of a direct download option within the app.

Defining Target Participants

The target participants for the user research were current users of the Natwest Mobile banking app, encompassing both Android and iOS users. I ensured a mix of different demographics to ensure diverse perspectives. This diversity helped to capture a wide range of experiences and opinions, ultimately leading to a more comprehensive understanding of user needs.

Survey Questions

By utilizing these survey questions, I successfully collected valuable data to inform the app redesign process and prioritize the areas that needed improvement based on user feedback.

Hypothesis

By addressing the identified pain points from user research and incorporating user-centric design principles, the redesigned Natwest Mobile banking app will lead to increased user satisfaction, improved app usage frequency, and higher adoption rates.

Rationale:

Resolving Biometric Login Issues: With 43% of users reporting biometric login challenges, resolving these issues will enhance the app's security and streamline the login process. By ensuring smooth and reliable biometric authentication, users will experience fewer frustrations and gain confidence in the app's security measures, encouraging them to use it more frequently.

Simplifying Navigation: The 21% of users who find the app's navigation complex will greatly benefit from a more intuitive and straightforward user interface. By simplifying the app's navigation, we can reduce user confusion, improve task completion times, and make the app more user-friendly, leading to increased usage and engagement.

Implementing Incoming Payment Notifications: As 21% of users expressed the need for push notifications for incoming payments, adding this feature will enhance financial management and user convenience. Real-time notifications will help users stay on top of their transactions and account activities, leading to better money management and a sense of control over their finances.

Enabling Direct Statements Download: Addressing the 14% of users bothered by the lack of a direct statements download option will enhance user convenience and streamline financial record-keeping. By enabling users to download statements directly within the app, we eliminate the need for alternative methods, making the app more self-sufficient and user-friendly.

By aligning the app redesign with the needs and preferences of the user personas, we anticipate a positive impact on user satisfaction. Improving the user experience in these critical areas will foster greater trust and loyalty among existing users and attract new users, leading to increased app adoption rates. Overall, the redesign aims to create an app that users will love to engage with, making the Natwest Mobile banking app the go-to choice for effortless and efficient banking on-the-go.

How Might We

"How might we?" questions are a great way to reframe the user feedback into actionable problem statements that can guide the design and development of solutions. I came up with five "How might we?" questions based on the NatWest user feedback:

How might we improve the biometric login experience to ensure smooth and reliable authentication for all users, addressing the frustration and inconvenience expressed by those facing biometric issues?

How might we enhance the app's navigation and menu design to make it more intuitive and user-friendly, especially for users who found the current navigation complicated and unfinished?

How might we implement incoming payments push notifications to promptly inform users about received money, providing a vital feature that some users consider essential for better financial management and peace of mind?

How might we offer users the ability to download and view their monthly statements directly from the app, eliminating the need to access the desktop site, and addressing the expressed desire for greater convenience and accessibility of financial information?

How might we optimize the app's performance and network connectivity to minimize network-related issues, ensuring a smoother user experience even under varying network conditions?

3. Ideate üí°

Information Architecture

During the ideation phase, I worked on revamping the information architecture of the Natwest Mobile banking app to create a more intuitive and user-friendly layout. The goal was to streamline the menu and incorporate new features that directly addressed the user pain points and desires identified during the research phase.

1. Information Architecture: Streamlined Menu

To simplify app navigation and make it more user-friendly, I redesigned the menu structure. The new information architecture focused on categorizing features logically, ensuring easy access to frequently used functions. The main menu was organized into clear sections, such as "Accounts," "Payments," "Cards," "Statements," and "Settings," making it easier for users to find what they need quickly.

2. Viewing and Downloading Statements

Under the "Statements" section, I added a dedicated feature that allows users to view and download their account statements directly within the app. Users can access their transaction history and select a specific time frame to generate and download the statement in PDF format. This feature provides users with a convenient way to access their financial records, eliminating the need to resort to alternative methods.

3. Card Details Management

Under the new "manage My Card" section, I introduced a feature that empowers users to manage their bank cards' details securely. Users can now view essential card information, such as card number, expiration date, and account balance. To ensure security, I implemented two-factor authentication, allowing users to access this sensitive information either by using their fingerprint or by entering their app password.

4. Access to Card PIN and Copy Card Details

Within the "Card Details Management" feature, users can now view their card's PIN securely, removing the need for face recognition for this purpose. Additionally, I added a "Copy Card Details" option, which allows users to copy their card information quickly for online transactions or reference without compromising security.

User Flow

To ensure a smooth and intuitive user experience, I created user flows for two critical interactions: logging into the Natwest Mobile banking app and viewing/downloading statements. These user flows were designed with the goal of simplifying the process and addressing the pain points identified during the research phase.
