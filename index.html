What I do

Design and Research

I'm a dedicated lifelong learner with a strong passion for UX, design, and technology.

My expertise lies in UX research, encompassing both qualitative and quantitative research methods, acquired through my studies with the Interaction Design Foundation and hands-on experience with clients during my freelance work.

I'm proficient in design thinking tools and methodologies, including customer journey mapping, co-creation, prototyping, and empathy mapping. When working, I consistently apply Jakob Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics and Don Norman's 6 principles of design.

I also draw inspiration from renowned design processes such as IDEO, Stanford's d.School, and the British Design Council's Double Diamond model.

I'm skilled in using software like Adobe Illustrator, Figma, Adobe XD, Photoshop and Microsoft Office. I'm an efficient and enthusiastic learner, always eager to expand my horisons, regardless of the subject matter.

I'm also deeply passionate about making a positive impact on the world, both in terms of improving the lives of disadvantaged individuals and protecting our planet.

Achievements

Proud member of the Tech-Fleet community, a nonprofit organization dedicated to promoting equitable access to tech careers. I work on real Agile teams with my peers to gain real-life experience and work on socially impactful projects.

Achieved a remarkable 98% total mark in the User Research – Methods and Best Practices certificate program from the Interaction Design Foundation.

Attained a top 10% distinction in the Data-Driven Design: Quantitative Research for UX course certificate, also from the Interaction Design Foundation.

Completed coursework at Birkbeck University, gaining hands-on experience in designing and building websites using HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

Participated in the "How to Design for Neurodiversity: Inclusive Content and UX" webinar hosted by The Interaction Design Foundation, expanding my knowledge of designing for neurodiverse individuals.

Earned a top 10% distinction in "The Brain and Technology: Brain Science in Interface Design" course offered by the Interaction Design Foundation.
